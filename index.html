Radioactive Waste for Different Reactors

A demo of radioactive.js

This plot compares radiation levels of waste from three different reactors over the next 10 billion years.

— Waste radiation from a standard Pressurized Water Reactor. The mixture of waste isotopes was determined from measurements at this German reactor.

— Waste radiation from a reactor with "three times less waste" than a PWR. This illustrates how useless the statement "three times less waste" is. The mixture of waste isotopes used here was determined from measurements at the same German reactor, but the amount of uranium-238 waste (more than 90% of the waste by weight) was reduced by a factor of three.

— Waste radiation from a hypothetical Actinide Burner Reactor. The starting mixture of waste isotopes was taken from measurements at the same German reactor, but with all actinides heavier than uranium removed, because they would be used up in the reactor.
